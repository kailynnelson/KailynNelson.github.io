Case study: Design for returns disruption

I helped the worldâ€™s leading brands to recover more value from their returned items.

The mission of Trove, an innovative circular commerce logistics startup, is to extend the lifespan of high-quality gear and apparel by operating profitable trade-in and resale programs at scale for top outdoor and luxury brands like Patagonia, lululemon, and Gucci. I joined the company in June 2022 to design the user experience (UX) and user interfaces (UIs) for their warehouse operations software products.

In November 2022, a market opportunity arose to disrupt returns management. A cross-functional team was assembled, inclusive of business leaders, warehouse operations experts, engineers and architects, and me, the product designer. My purpose in this team was to deliver a lean, vetted, and feasible design prototype in a high-stakes, fast-paced environment so that our team could test the market as quickly and thoughtfully as possible.

This is the story of how I used the power of design thinking and artifacts to navigate through ambiguity, drive stakeholder alignment, and deliver a crystal-clear user experience for our pilot.

Key takeaways

My role

  • Served as the owner of the design program and artifact delivery
  • Partnered with cross-functional teammates, from discovery to launch
  • Facilitated alignment and design-thinking workshops with executives and other contributors
  • Conducted a usability study with 13 subject-matter experts (SMEs) using the RITE method
  • Delivered an interactive Figma prototype that satisfied user and business needs
  • Produced a visual style guide based on Material UI to get us to launch faster
  • Stewarded my designs forward with engineers and architects, from tech spec to shipped
